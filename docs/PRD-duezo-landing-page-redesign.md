# Duezo Landing Page Redesign PRD

## 1. Summary

Redesign the Duezo landing page into a premium, iPhone-first product page that drives App Store downloads by doing three things far better than the current site:

1. Communicate what Duezo actually is in under 5 seconds.
2. Make the product feel visually premium and distinctly iOS-native.
3. Build trust through specificity, product proof, and consistent messaging.

This redesign is not just a visual cleanup. The bigger problem is that Duezo currently tells different product stories across different pages. A prettier page will not fix that. The redesign must start by establishing a single source of truth for pricing, core features, onboarding model, and positioning.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

The current homepage is clear enough to understand at a basic level, but it looks and reads like a generic SaaS landing page instead of a premium consumer app page. It undersells the product in four ways:

- Weak differentiation: the homepage does not fully lean into the strongest wedge: bill tracking without bank linking.
- Too feature-list driven: it reads like a checklist instead of a convincing narrative.
- Too little product proof: there is not enough visual storytelling, interaction, or trust-building detail.
- Inconsistent messaging across the site: pricing, ingestion methods, and free/pro messaging conflict across pages.

Result: the site is “fine,” but not memorable, not premium, and not as conversion-oriented as it should be.

---

## 3. Current State Audit

### 3.1 What the current homepage gets right

- Clear promise: avoid missed bill payments.
- Strongly relevant differentiator: no bank linking required.
- Reasonable section structure: hero, feature grid, setup steps, CTA.
- The actual app UI looks good and is visually distinct.

### 3.2 What the current homepage gets wrong

#### A. The core messaging is too generic

The current hero language is broad and vague. It sounds like a generic fintech or productivity app, not a focused bill-tracking product.

Current direction: “Your Bills, On Autopilot”

Why this is weak:

- “Autopilot” implies automation and possibly autopay.
- The App Store description positions Duezo as manual-first and about staying in control.
- That mismatch creates friction and subtle distrust.

#### B. The page behaves like a feature page, not a product story

The current flow is basically:

- hero
- feature bullets
- pricing teaser
- 3-step how it works
- CTA

That is functional, but it does not create momentum. It lacks:

- a sharp problem statement
- a believable “why Duezo” argument
- clear comparison against full-budgeting apps
- trust and founder proof
- FAQ for objections

#### C. The product proof is too thin

Duezo’s app UI appears more polished than the website experience around it. The landing page should make the app feel premium through:

- multiple product screens
- motion / recorded flows
- visual callouts tied to actual use cases
- stronger screenshot hierarchy

Right now the product is stronger than the page.

#### D. The site has inconsistent product truth

Before redesigning visuals, fix the contradictions.

Current inconsistencies observed across public pages:

- Homepage says free to download with optional Pro.
- App Store lists specific Duezo Pro pricing tiers.
- About page says “Every feature, no limits, no cost.”
- Comparison pages show different pricing again.
- Homepage emphasizes Quick Add + photo scan.
- About / comparison / Prism pages emphasize AI email parsing or Gmail scanning.

This is the biggest trust leak on the site.

## 4. Product Truth Cleanup (P0)

Before any redesign ships, define and document the canonical answers to these:

### 4.1 Pricing

Pick one source of truth and update every public page:

- Free plan: what exactly is included?
- Pro plan: what exactly is included?
- Monthly price
- Annual price
- Lifetime price, if still offered
- Trial or no trial

### 4.2 Bill ingestion methods

Decide which of these are real, current product features:
- Quick Add / vendor autocomplete
- AI photo scan
- Email parsing
- Gmail scanning
- Manual entry

If multiple are real, explain them clearly as separate methods. If not, remove stale claims everywhere.

### 4.3 Positioning

Choose one positioning statement and keep it consistent:

- Is Duezo a manual-first bill tracker?
- Is it an AI-assisted bill tracker?
- Is it a Gmail-driven bill detector?
- Is it a privacy-first alternative to bank-linked finance apps?

It can be more than one of these, but the homepage hero must lead with one primary wedge.

### 4.4 Trust claims

Only publish claims that can be defended.

Examples that need validation before surfacing prominently:

- user counts
- “thousands of former Prism users”
- savings claims
- setup time claims
- comparison pricing claims versus competitors

No fake proof. No inflated proof. Specificity beats hype.

## 5. Goals

### Primary goal

Increase qualified clicks from the landing page to the App Store.

### Secondary goals

- Improve clarity of what Duezo does.
- Make the brand feel premium and intentional.
- Improve trust for privacy-conscious users.
- Create a homepage that matches the quality of the app.
- Create a reusable design system for future pages (comparison pages, blog landing, FAQ, feature pages).

### Non-goals

- Building a general marketing site for every possible audience.
- Turning Duezo into a broad budgeting app brand.
- Stuffing every feature into the homepage.
- Relying on fake testimonials or empty social proof.

---

## 6. Target Audience

### Primary audience

People who:

- pay recurring bills on iPhone
- have been burned by late fees or forgotten due dates
- do not want a full budgeting system
- do not want to link bank accounts
- want a simple, visually clear system for due dates

### High-intent sub-audiences

- Former Prism users
- People frustrated by overbuilt apps like YNAB / Rocket Money / full finance dashboards
- Privacy-conscious users who reject bank linking
- People who already budget elsewhere and only need bill tracking

### Jobs to be done

- “Make sure I never forget a bill again.”
- “Show me what is due next, fast.”
- “Help me add bills without a bunch of setup friction.”
- “Give me reminders without demanding bank credentials.”

---

## 7. Messaging Strategy

### 7.1 Positioning

Recommended positioning:

> Duezo is the focused iPhone bill tracker for people who want clear due-date visibility without linking their bank accounts.

This is stronger than trying to be “smart finance,” “money management,” or “autopilot.”

### 7.2 Core message pillars

1. Never miss a bill again
   - The core outcome.
2. No bank linking required
   - The clearest wedge versus bigger finance apps.
3. Beautiful visual countdowns
   - This is the product’s most distinctive UI concept.
4. Fast setup
   - Quick Add, scan, or whichever ingestion method is truly current.
5. Built for iPhone
   - Lean into native quality instead of pretending to be cross-platform.
6. Built by someone who actually had this problem
   - Founder story is a trust asset.

### 7.3 Messaging to avoid

Avoid leading with:

- “autopilot”
- “all-in-one finance” language
- vague productivity copy
- finance-bro language
- fake urgency or inflated user-count claims

### 7.4 Recommended homepage headline territory

Preferred headline options:

1. Never miss a bill again.
2. The bill tracker for people who hate bank linking.
3. Your bills. One clear countdown.
4. Stop paying late fees because you forgot.

Recommended subhead:

> Track bills with visual countdowns, reminders, AI-assisted entry, calendar view, and widgets — built for iPhone and designed to work without linking your bank.

## 8. Visual Direction

### 8.1 Design goal

The page should feel like a premium iPhone product launch page, not a startup template.

### 8.2 Visual principles
- Dark-first aesthetic with controlled purple glow pulled from the app.
- Heavy use of actual app UI in device frames.
- Large spacing, strong hierarchy, minimal clutter.
- Crisp typography and short, high-confidence copy.
- Motion used sparingly and deliberately.
- Zero stock photos in core product sections.

### 8.3 Visual style references (for direction, not copying)

Think:

- Apple product-page discipline
- Copilot/Monarch-level product polish
- More product UI, less lifestyle fluff
- More confidence, fewer words

### 8.4 Required visual ingredients

- 3–6 polished iPhone screenshots from the latest product version
- 1 hero motion asset (looped screen recording or short product animation)
- clean iconography for trust chips and feature callouts
- consistent color tokens pulled from the app
- stronger device framing and depth than current single-screenshot layout

## 9. Homepage Information Architecture

## Section 1 — Sticky navigation

### Purpose

Keep CTA visible and keep navigation simple.

### Requirements

- Logo on left
- Links: Features, Why Duezo, Pricing, FAQ
- Right side CTA: App Store badge or “Download for iPhone”
- Optional Sign In link should be visually de-emphasized

### Notes

Do not let nav compete with the primary conversion action.

---

## Section 2 — Hero

### Purpose

Explain the product and drive first CTA click.

### Content requirements

- One sharp headline
- One clear subhead
- Primary CTA: Download on the App Store
- Secondary CTA: Watch 20-second demo / See how it works
- 3 trust chips directly under CTA:
  - No bank linking
  - Built for iPhone
  - Free to download

### Visual requirements

- Multi-screen iPhone composition or animated product demo
- The dashboard countdown screen must be visible immediately
- Secondary screens should show scan/add flow and calendar/widgets

### Copy recommendation

Headline: Never miss a bill again.

Subhead: Duezo helps you track every bill with clear countdowns, reminders, and fast AI-assisted entry — without linking your bank account.

### Why this works

It is sharper, more believable, and more aligned with the App Store than “Your Bills, On Autopilot.”

---

## Section 3 — The problem with current alternatives

### Purpose

Create contrast and show why Duezo exists.

### Content structure

Short section with 3 cards:

- Budgeting apps are overkill
  - You wanted bill reminders, not a full financial operating system.
- Bank linking is a trust hurdle
  - Many users do not want to hand over account credentials just to track due dates.
- Calendar apps are too dumb
  - They remind you of dates, but they do not provide a bill-specific workflow.

### Design note

This section should be visually minimal and read fast.

---

## Section 4 — Product showcase

### Purpose

Show the product, not just tell.

### Recommended modules

#### Module A — Countdown dashboard
- Show urgency-based visual design
- Explain “see what’s due next instantly”

#### Module B — Add a bill fast
- Show Quick Add / photo scan / whichever input method is current
- Explain frictionless setup

#### Module C — Calendar + reminders
- Show planning and due-date visibility
- Explain staying ahead of bills, not reacting late

#### Module D — Widgets / history / themes
- Keep this as a lighter module
- Support the premium feel of the app

### Requirements

- Each module should pair one screen with one insight
- Do not dump 10 tiny feature cards on screen
- Keep copy short: one headline, one sentence, one visual proof point

---

## Section 5 — Why users trust Duezo

### Purpose

Turn founder story and privacy angle into credibility.

### Content structure

#### Option A — Founder card

Use the strongest parts of the About page story:

- paid a late fee despite having the money
- hated needing to link bank accounts
- built the app he wanted
- bootstrapped / indie-built / direct support

#### Option B — Product principles

3 trust pillars:

- No bank linking required
- Focused on bills, not bloat
- Built by an indie founder who uses it

### Design note
This is where a founder photo can help if available. If not, use a refined text-forward card.

---

## Section 6 — Pricing

### Purpose

Stop being vague. Make the offer understandable.

### Requirements

- Show real pricing that matches the App Store / current product truth
- Clearly separate Free vs Pro
- Highlight the most sensible plan
- Explain what unlocks with Pro
- If lifetime is real, show it clearly
- Add a small regional pricing disclaimer if needed

### Suggested layout

- Free card
- Pro monthly card
- Pro yearly card (default emphasis)
- Optional lifetime card

### Important

This section must match:

- App Store listing
- in-app purchase reality
- comparison pages
- FAQ

No contradictions.

---

## Section 7 — FAQ

### Purpose

Handle objections and reduce hesitation.

### Required questions

- Do I need to link my bank account?
- Is Duezo free?
- What does Pro unlock?
- Does Duezo work on Android?
- Can I add bills manually?
- Does Duezo support scan / email import / both?
- How do reminders work?
- Is my data private?

### Notes

This section matters more than people think. It prevents bounce from unresolved uncertainty.

---

## Section 8 — Final CTA

### Purpose

Close with a clean conversion block.

### Recommended copy

Ready to stop missing bills?

Download Duezo for iPhone and set up your first bill in minutes.

CTA: Download on the App Store

### Visual direction

- big, clean, minimal
- no extra clutter
- should feel like the end of a product launch page

## 10. Social Proof Strategy

Duezo should not fake maturity.

Current public proof appears limited. That means the homepage should use a smart trust stack instead of pretending to have giant scale.

### Recommended proof order

1. Product proof (screenshots, motion, specificity)
2. Clear privacy position
3. Founder story
4. Real App Store reviews, only if you have enough to feature them honestly
5. Press / comparison / user numbers only if verifiable

### Avoid

- giant fake testimonial carousels
- “trusted by thousands” without proof
- anonymous quotes with no source

---

## 11. Copy Guidelines

### Tone

- direct
- specific
- calm confidence
- no fintech jargon
- no fake hype
- no fluffy self-importance

### Writing rules

- keep headlines outcome-first
- use short paragraphs
- earn trust with specifics
- do not describe the app like a productivity toy
- do not overuse “AI” unless the feature actually matters to user value

### Example style

Bad:

> Revolutionize your financial life with the smartest bill management platform.

Good:

> See every bill that’s due next — without linking your bank account.

---

## 12. Functional Requirements

### Core

- Fully responsive, mobile-first
- Fast loading on mobile
- Sticky CTA on mobile and desktop
- App Store badge in hero and footer
- Clean anchor navigation
- Easy screenshot replacement as product evolves

### Analytics

Track at minimum:

- Hero CTA click
- Secondary CTA click
- Scroll depth
- Pricing section view
- FAQ opens
- Final CTA click

### App Store attribution

Use campaign-tagged App Store links so homepage-to-App Store performance can actually be measured.

### SEO / metadata

- Strong title and meta description aligned with bill tracking + no bank linking
- Open Graph image that looks premium
- FAQ schema if FAQ section is included
- SoftwareApplication schema if appropriate

---

## 13. Accessibility Requirements

- Contrast must remain strong in dark mode
- Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences
- All screenshots need descriptive alt text
- Tap targets must be large on mobile
- Heading structure must be semantic
- FAQ accordion must be keyboard accessible

## 15. Open Questions
These must be answered before final copy lock:

1. What is the exact live pricing today?
2. Does the app currently support Gmail scanning / email parsing, photo scan, or both?
3. What is included in Free vs Pro?
4. Is there a trial?
5. Do you want Sign In visible in the primary nav?
6. Do you have real usage stats or customer quotes worth surfacing?
7. Is the target audience primarily former Prism users, general bill trackers, or privacy-conscious users coming from broader finance apps?

## 17. Acceptance Criteria

The redesign is successful when a first-time visitor can answer all of these within 5–10 seconds:

- What is Duezo?
- Is it for bill tracking specifically?
- Why would I use this instead of a budgeting app?
- Do I need to link my bank account?
- Is it built for iPhone?
- What does it cost?
- What should I do next?

And the page should feel:

- premium
- focused
- believable
- visually tighter than the current site
- aligned with the app itself

---

## 18. Brutal Summary

The current homepage is not terrible. It is just not sharp enough.

The app has a stronger product story than the page:

- founder-driven
- privacy-conscious
- bills-only
- visually distinctive
- iPhone-native

That is the angle.

Do not redesign this as another broad “money management” site.
Do not hide pricing.
Do not keep contradictory copy across pages.
Do not let a generic hero waste the strongest positioning you have.

Build the page around one clear idea:

> Duezo helps you never miss a bill again — without bank linking, without budgeting bloat, and without making the product feel complicated.
